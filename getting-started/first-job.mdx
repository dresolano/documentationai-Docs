## Creating and scheduling your first job

Jobs are the individual work assignments your team completes for clients. Each job has a title, client, assigned team members, date, time, and duration. You can also set up recurring jobs for ongoing work.

## Prerequisites

<Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
  Sign in as an **Owner** or **Manager** before you create a job. You should also have at least one client and one team member added to your company. Techs cannot create jobs.
</Callout>

## Creating a job

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Jobs page" title-type="p">
    From the Triage dashboard, open the **Jobs** page. This is where you can view and manage all your jobs.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Create Job" title-type="p">
    Select **Create Job** to open the job form.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter a job title and description" title-type="p">
    Add a **Job Title** first. The title is required and helps your team quickly identify the work.

    Add a **Job Description** if you want to include scope details or extra context for the team. The description is optional.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a client" title-type="p">
    Choose the client for this job from the client dropdown.

    If the client is not in the list yet, click **+ New Client** to add one without leaving the form.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assign team members" title-type="p">
    Select one or more people from your company to work on the job.

    Hold <kbd>Ctrl</kbd> on Windows or <kbd>Cmd</kbd> on Mac to select multiple team members. The list shows each person's name and role.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the date and time" title-type="p">
    Pick the job date. Then choose a start time if you want to schedule the visit at a specific time.

    Time is optional. When you set a time, choose from 15-minute intervals.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose a duration" title-type="p">
    Select how long the job should take. Options range from **30 minutes** to **Full Day**.

    Duration helps you plan the day and makes the job easier to place on the schedule board.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add notes" title-type="p">
    Use **Notes** for details your team needs on site, such as gate codes, parking instructions, or special access information.

    Notes are optional, but they help your team arrive prepared.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Save" title-type="p">
    Click **Save** to create the job.

    After the form submits, you will see a success message and be taken to the job details page. New jobs start with a **Scheduled** status unless you choose a different status in the form.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Setting up recurring jobs

If the job repeats on a regular schedule, set it up as a recurring job instead of creating each visit manually.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Turn on recurring settings" title-type="p">
    On the job form, check **Make this a recurring job**. This shows the recurring options.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose a frequency" title-type="p">
    Select how often the job should repeat: **Daily**, **Weekly**, **Bi-Weekly**, or **Monthly**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select days of the week when needed" title-type="p">
    If you choose **Weekly** or **Bi-Weekly**, select the days of the week when the job should repeat.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Use the date as the first job" title-type="p">
    The date on the form is the first scheduled job.

    NoSavvy automatically generates future jobs based on the recurring schedule you set.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Job statuses

Jobs move through a simple status lifecycle as the work gets scheduled, confirmed, completed, and closed.

- **Scheduled** — The job is created and on the calendar.
- **Ready** — The job is confirmed and ready for the visit.
- **In Progress** — Your crew is on site or actively working.
- **Completed** — The work is finished.
- **Archived** — The job is hidden from active views.

## Using the schedule board

The schedule board gives you a visual view of jobs by team member. You can drag and drop job cards between staff columns to reassign work, or drag a job to the queue to remove its assignment. This is especially useful when you need to make same-day schedule changes.
